Conclusion

           The "What's Your Warrior?" advertising campaign launched by the United States Army in 2019 plays on the individualistic traits of the United States by emphasizing the unique strengths and interests of potential recruits. In Hofstede's cultural analysis, the United States scores high on individualism, which means that individuals are expected to be self-reliant, assertive, and focus on their own goals and interests. The "What's Your Warrior?" campaign speaks to this cultural value by presenting the Army as a place where individuals can pursue their own interests and develop their unique strengths. The campaign offers personalized messaging based on potential recruits' interests and strengths, emphasizing that there are many different roles and opportunities within the Army. By tapping into the individualistic traits of the United States, the campaign seeks to attract a new generation of recruits who value personal growth and self-determination. (Hofstede Insights)

           While the "What's Your Warrior?" advertising campaign launched by the United States Army in 2019 aimed to attract a new generation of recruits, the effectiveness of the campaign may be called into question due to the fact that the total number of Army personnel has decreased since the ad began airing. Despite the personalized messaging and emphasis on individual strengths, the number of new recruits has not met the Army's goals in recent years. It is possible that the campaign did not resonate with its intended audience or that other factors, such as economic opportunities or changing attitudes towards military service, played a greater role in the decline in recruitment. Nonetheless, the decrease in total Army personnel suggests that the "What's Your Warrior?" campaign may not have been as effective as the Army had hoped in recruiting and retaining new personnel.(Statista 2023)
