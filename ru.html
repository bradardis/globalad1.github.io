Conclusion

           The Russian military ad "Real Man" can be analyzed through the lens of Hofstede's cultural analysis to understand its resonance with Russian culture. Russia is considered to be a culture with high power distance, which means that there is a significant power gap between those in positions of authority and those who are not. The ad emphasizes the idea of strength, dominance, and physical toughness, which are important traits in Russian culture. The ad portrays the military as an institution that provides a sense of order and stability, which is valued in a culture with a relatively high level of uncertainty avoidance. The emphasis on physical strength and discipline also appeals to a collectivistic culture, where the group is prioritized over the individual. The "Real Man" ad also taps into the concept of masculinity, which is highly valued in Russian culture. The ad portrays the military as a place where men can prove their strength and courage, and emphasizes the idea of "real" men who are tough and unafraid. This emphasis on traditional gender roles is consistent with the relatively low level of gender egalitarianism in Russian culture. Overall, the "Real Man" ad speaks to the cultural values of strength, discipline, and masculinity that are highly valued in Russian culture, and highlights the important role that the military plays in reinforcing these values. (Hofstede 2023)

           Russian military propaganda has been effective in recruiting new servicemen, as evidenced by the increase in numbers from 2018 to 2023. The propaganda often emphasizes patriotism, nationalism, and the importance of serving one's country. In a culture that values loyalty to the state and the collective, these messages resonate with many Russians. Additionally, the propaganda frequently emphasizes the need for a strong military to protect Russia's sovereignty, which taps into a sense of national pride and the desire to protect the country from external threats. It's worth noting, however, that the accuracy of Russian military enrollment numbers is often questioned. There have been accusations that the Russian government inflates the number of new recruits in order to create an illusion of strength and to hide the true state of the military. Furthermore, conscription remains a key part of the Russian military recruitment process, which means that many new recruits may not have volunteered to serve. Despite these issues, it's clear that the propaganda has played a role in encouraging many Russians to join the military and to view military service as an honorable and important part of their lives. (Statista 2023)
