Conclusion

           The Chinese military advertisement "We Are Here" can be analyzed through the lens of Hofstede's cultural dimensions to understand its resonance with Chinese culture. China is considered a collectivist culture, where the group is prioritized over the individual. The ad emphasizes the importance of duty and sacrifice for the greater good, which is consistent with the collectivist cultural values. It portrays the military as an institution that provides a sense of belonging and purpose, which is important in a culture that places great emphasis on social harmony and stability. Additionally, the ad highlights the role of the military in protecting the nation and its people, which taps into a sense of national pride and loyalty that is highly valued in Chinese culture. Furthermore, China has a relatively high power distance, which means that there is a significant power gap between those in positions of authority and those who are not. The ad emphasizes the idea of discipline, obedience, and respect for authority, which are all highly valued in Chinese culture. The ad portrays the military as an institution that provides structure and guidance, which is important in a culture that values hierarchy and order. The ad also emphasizes the idea of sacrifice and hard work, which are important in a culture that places a high value on perseverance and diligence. Overall, the "We Are Here" ad speaks to the cultural values of duty, sacrifice, and obedience that are highly valued in Chinese culture, and highlights the important role that the military plays in reinforcing these values.

           China's military has traditionally relied on a large pool of conscripts to maintain its ranks, but in recent years, the number of people enlisting in the military has been declining. This is despite the Chinese government's efforts to promote military service through collectivist advertising and propaganda. One possible explanation for this decline is that young people in China may have different priorities than previous generations. With a growing middle class and an emphasis on education, many young Chinese people may see military service as less desirable than other career paths. Additionally, the increasing use of technology and automation in the military may be making traditional military roles less appealing to some potential recruits. The Chinese government has also faced criticism for its treatment of military personnel, which may be contributing to the decline in enlistment. In recent years, there have been reports of low pay, poor living conditions, and mistreatment of soldiers in the Chinese military. This has led to dissatisfaction among some soldiers and may be discouraging potential recruits from enlisting. Despite these challenges, the Chinese government is still investing heavily in its military and has set a goal of modernizing its armed forces by 2035. It remains to be seen whether the government's efforts to promote military service and improve conditions for soldiers will be enough to reverse the decline in enlistment and meet its goals for military modernization.(Statista 2023)
