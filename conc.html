Hofstede's Arms Race

After analyzing the military advertisements of four different countries through the lens of Hofstede's cultural analysis, it is clear that there is no direct correlation between these ads and military enlistment rates. Although the ads do reflect the cultural values of their respective countries, it appears that the proximity of the country to ongoing conflicts plays a much larger role in military enlistment. For example, Germany and Russia are located in Europe, which is currently experiencing conflict, and both have seen an increase in military enlistment despite having different cultural values and approaches to military advertising. On the other hand, the United States and China are not currently engaged in an active war, and both have experienced a decline in military enlistment despite their unique approaches to military advertising. Therefore, while Hofstede's cultural analysis provides insight into the values and cultural differences that shape military advertising, it is clear that other factors play a larger role in determining military enlistment rates.
